## 1. Whale Concentration Is Systemic, Not Episodic

Whale concentration (top 10% of donors controlling majority of funding) is a structural feature of Octant, not a temporary anomaly:

| Epoch | Donations | Unique Donors | Total ETH | Whale Concentration | Max Single Donation |
|-------|-----------|---------------|-----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 3 | 1,937 | 285 | 25.48 ETH | **98.6%** | 4.59 ETH |
| 4 | 1,602 | 279 | 4.79 ETH | **92.8%** | 0.25 ETH |
| 5 | 1,902 | 422 | 17.63 ETH | **97.9%** | 2.05 ETH |
| 6 | 1,978 | 455 | 14.31 ETH | **97.2%** | 1.84 ETH |

**Insight:** Despite growing donor counts (279 to 455, +63% over 3 epochs), whale concentration remains above 92% in every epoch. The median donation is ~0.0001 ETH (~$0.25) while the max is 1,000-40,000x larger. This means quadratic funding matching is being driven by a handful of large allocators, not broad community preference.

**What this means for Octant:** The mechanism is working as designed (QF amplifies small donations), but the *signal quality* is dominated by whales. Projects favored by top donors receive disproportionate matching regardless of broad community support.

## 5. Mechanism Design Has Dramatic Redistribution Effects

Simulating 4 funding mechanisms on Epoch 5 real data:

| Mechanism | Gini | Top Project Share | Above Threshold |
|-----------|------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Standard QF (current) | 0.399 | 10.5% | 24/30 |
| Capped QF (10%) | 0.375 | 12.8% | 26/30 |
| Equal Weight | 0.164 | 4.9% | 30/30 |
| Trust-Weighted QF | ~0.37 | ~10% | ~26/30 |

**Key findings:**
- **Equal Weight** would increase the smallest project's funding by **3,105%** but makes sybil attacks trivially valuable (each fake identity = 1 full vote)
- **Capped QF** reduces whale influence (-0.024 Gini) while preserving preference signal
- **Trust-Weighted QF** (our novel mechanism) modulates matching by donor diversity scores, penalizing whale-dominated projects up to 50% while preserving organic support signals

**The fundamental trade-off:** Equality vs. preference signal quality vs. sybil resistance. No mechanism optimizes all three. Trust-Weighted QF is our proposed balance point.

## Methodology

All findings are generated from live Octant API data using deterministic algorithms:
- **Whale concentration**: Top 10% of donors' share of total funding
- **Shannon entropy**: Normalized information entropy of donation distribution (0 = single donor, 1 = uniform)
- **Jaccard similarity**: Intersection/union of donor sets between projects (>0.7 = coordinated)
- **Union-find clustering**: Graph-based donor grouping by funding pattern similarity
- **Gini coefficient**: Standard inequality measure (0 = equal, 1 = one entity gets all)
- **Coefficient of variation**: Standard deviation / mean of cross-epoch funding (lower = more consistent)

Qualitative assessments are generated by Claude Opus 4.6, fed with all quantitative evidence from steps 1-7 of the pipeline.
